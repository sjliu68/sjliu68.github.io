Shengjie Liu received the B.S. degree in Geographical Information Science from Sun Yat-sen University, Guangzhou, China, in 2019. He then worked briefly as a Remote Sensing Engineer at OneSpace Technology Co., Ltd., Chongqing, China. He is currently a Research Assistant with the Department of Physics, The University of Hong Kong, where he studies artificial light at night (ALAN) and light pollution in Hong Kong. 

His research interests include remote sensing image processing, machine learning (deep learning and multitask learning), hyperspectral data analysis, and night lights.
